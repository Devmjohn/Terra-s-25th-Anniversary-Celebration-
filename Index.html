About Terra

Terra, launched in 1999, is a pioneering NASA satellite dedicated to studying our home planet. Orbiting Earth in a sun-synchronous path, it carries five powerful instruments that work together to observe land, water, ice, clouds, and the energy balance that drives our climate. For 25 years, Terra has monitored wildfires, volcanic eruptions, melting glaciers, and changing forests, providing data that helps scientists, policymakers, and communities worldwide. Originally designed for a 6-year mission, Terra continues to serve as one of the longest-running and most impactful Earth science missions ever
