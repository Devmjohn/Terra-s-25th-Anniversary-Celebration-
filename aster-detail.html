About ASTER

The Advanced Spaceborne Thermal Emission and Reflection Radiometer (ASTER) is a Japanese sensor aboard NASA's Terra satellite. ASTER creates detailed maps of land surface temperature, elevation, reflectance, and emissivity. These maps are used by scientists and decision-makers to better understand the local and regional effects of climate change, monitor land use patterns, assess natural hazards including volcanic eruptions and wildfires, and measure ecosystem changes.

ASTER has been instrumental in creating the most detailed digital elevation model (DEM) of Earth's surface, covering 99% of the planet's landmass. This data is crucial for understanding terrain, water flow, landslide risk, and infrastructure planning.
