About MODIS

The Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS) is one of the most comprehensive Earth observing instruments ever deployed. Flying aboard NASA's Terra and Aqua satellites, MODIS views the entire Earth's surface every 1-2 days, acquiring data in 36 spectral bands ranging from visible to thermal infrared wavelengths.

MODIS provides a wide range of critical data for studying global dynamics and processes on land, in the oceans, and in the atmosphere. Its observations help scientists understand changes in Earth's climate system, including cloud formation, ocean temperature, ice cover, vegetation health, wildfire activity, and atmospheric aerosols. This comprehensive data set has become essential for climate research, weather forecasting, and environmental monitoring worldwide.
