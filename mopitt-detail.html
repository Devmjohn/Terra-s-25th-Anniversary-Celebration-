About MOPITT

The Measurements of Pollution in the Troposphere (MOPITT) is a Canadian instrument aboard NASA's Terra satellite. MOPITT measures atmospheric carbon monoxide (CO) and methane (CH4) concentrations in the troposphere, providing crucial data for understanding air quality, atmospheric chemistry, and climate patterns.

Since its launch in 1999, MOPITT has been monitoring pollution transport, biomass burning emissions, and urban air quality on a global scale. This long-term dataset helps scientists track pollution trends, validate atmospheric models, and assess the impact of human activities on air quality and climate change.
